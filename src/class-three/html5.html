HTML5

What is HTML5?

HTML5 is the latest standard for HTML approved by the w3c.

  • Replaces both HTML 4, XHTML, and the HTML DOM Level 2.
  • Designed to deliver rich media.
  • HTML5 is cross platform.

<header>

Represents a group of introductory or navigational aids.

<nav>

A section of a page that links to other pages or to parts within the page: a section with navigation links.

<aside>

A portion of a page that consists of content that is tangentially related to the content around it.

<footer>

Represents a footer for its nearest sectioning content or sectioning root element.

<article>

A self-contained composition in a document, page, application, or site, which is intended to be independently distributable or reusable.

<section>

A generic section of a document, i.e., a thematic grouping of content, typically with a heading.

<time>

A data type denotes time dimensions expressed in seconds or milliseconds.

<time datetime="2014-06-04">Today</time>
